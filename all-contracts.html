Independent Contractor Agreement

This Independent Contractor Agreement (“Agreement”) is entered into by and between MTN HLTH (“Company”) and the undersigned Contractor as of the date of electronic signature. This Agreement governs the terms under which Contractor provides professional clinical and related services to the Company as a 1099 independent contractor.

1. Engagement

Company engages Contractor to provide functional medicine, hormone optimization, wellness, and mid-level clinical services within Contractor’s professional scope of practice. Contractor agrees to perform such services in accordance with applicable federal, state, and local laws, professional standards, and Company policies, protocols, and expectations.

2. Independent Contractor Status

Contractor is an independent contractor, not an employee, partner, agent, or representative of Company. Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed as creating an employment relationship. Contractor is solely responsible for all federal and state taxes, insurance, licensure costs, certifications, equipment, continuing education, and business expenses.

Contractor acknowledges that Company does not provide employee benefits, including health insurance, retirement benefits, unemployment insurance, workers’ compensation, or paid leave.

3. Compensation

Contractor shall be compensated according to the revenue-share structure, fixed fee schedule, or percentage-based arrangement outlined in the Compensation Addendum. Compensation may be updated or renegotiated at any time with written mutual agreement.

Payments are issued on a monthly basis following verification of completed visits, documentation, and billable items. Contractor agrees to submit invoices if requested and acknowledges that compensation received is considered independent-contractor income for tax purposes.

4. Duties & Responsibilities

Contractor agrees to perform the following duties:

  • Provide patient care through Company-approved telemedicine platforms or in-person, if applicable
  • Maintain accurate, timely, and complete clinical documentation
  • Follow MTN HLTH clinical protocols for hormones, nutrition, wellness, and functional medicine
  • Order labs, prescriptions, imaging, and treatments within licensure scope and Company guidelines
  • Use Company systems (EHR, Spruce, Google Workspace, etc.) correctly and securely
  • Respond to patient messages within Company-defined response windows
  • Participate in internal meetings, training sessions, and case reviews as requested
  • Practice in compliance with all telemedicine regulations for each state of operation
  • Comply with Company policies, SOPs, workflows, and medical quality standards

5. Clinical Documentation Requirements

Contractor shall complete clinical notes, charting, and updates within one (1) business day of each patient encounter unless otherwise approved. Failure to complete timely documentation may result in delayed payment.

6. Tools, Software, and Systems

Company will grant Contractor access to the following systems as needed:

  • Electronic Health Record (EHR)
  • Spruce secure messaging
  • Google Workspace (Company domain)
  • Telemedicine platforms and scheduling tools
  • Internal templates, SOPs, clinical frameworks, and automations

Contractor agrees to use these systems only for MTN HLTH business, to maintain security, and to follow HIPAA/HITECH compliance requirements at all times.

7. Confidentiality & HIPAA

Contractor shall maintain strict confidentiality of all PHI and Company proprietary information. Contractor will sign and comply with the Company’s Business Associate Agreement (BAA), HIPAA/PHI Confidentiality Agreement, and Security Rule Attestation.

Contractor shall use only Company-approved secure systems and shall not store or transmit PHI using personal email, SMS, or non-secure platforms.

8. Proprietary Information & Intellectual Property

All Company-created systems, templates, workflows, software, documents, patient resources, internal spreadsheets, GitHub code, Wellness Plan structures, automations, and clinical protocols remain the sole property of MTN HLTH.

Contractor shall not reproduce, modify, share, or use Company intellectual property for any purpose outside the performance of services under this Agreement. Contractor shall not create or operate a competing clinic using Company’s proprietary materials.

9. Non-Solicitation

For two (2) years following termination, Contractor shall not directly or indirectly:

  • Solicit or attempt to solicit any patient of Company
  • Induce Company employees or contractors to leave the Company
  • Use Company patient lists, leads, or contact data for personal gain
  • Market competing services to active or former Company patients

10. Limited Non-Competition

For one (1) year following termination, Contractor shall not operate or work in a business that uses Company’s proprietary methods, pricing structures, workflows, templates, marketing materials, or internal systems. This clause protects trade secrets and is not intended to restrict Contractor from practicing medicine generally.

11. Term & Termination

This Agreement remains in effect until terminated by either party. Either party may terminate with thirty (30) days written notice. Company may terminate immediately for material breach, loss of licensure, misconduct, or legal/regulatory violations.

12. Insurance Requirements

Contractor shall maintain professional liability (malpractice) insurance with coverage limits acceptable to the Company and provide proof of coverage upon request. Contractor is responsible for any required tail coverage.

13. Licensure & Credentialing

Contractor warrants that they hold all required licenses, certifications, prescriptive authority, DEA registration (where applicable), and credentials. Contractor agrees to notify Company immediately of any investigations, restrictions, or disciplinary actions.

14. Indemnification

Contractor agrees to indemnify and hold harmless Company, its owners, and affiliates against any liability, damages, or claims arising from Contractor’s negligence, errors, omissions, or violations of law.

15. Records & Data Ownership

All patient records, documentation, communications, and work product generated while providing services for Company are the exclusive property of MTN HLTH.

16. Return of Property

Upon termination, Contractor shall immediately return all Company property, including devices, documents, credentials, spreadsheets, templates, and digital materials, and shall delete any stored copies.

17. Dispute Resolution

Disputes shall first be addressed through good-faith negotiation. If unresolved, the parties agree to binding arbitration in the State of Utah.

18. Governing Law

This Agreement is governed by the laws of the State of Utah.

19. Entire Agreement

This Agreement, including all referenced addenda, constitutes the entire understanding between the parties and supersedes all prior agreements.

By signing electronically below, Contractor acknowledges that they have read, understood, and agree to be bound by the terms of this Independent Contractor Agreement.

HIPAA Business Associate Agreement

This Business Associate Agreement (“Agreement”) is entered into by and between MTN HLTH (“Covered Entity”) and the undersigned Contractor (“Business Associate”) as of the date of electronic signature. This Agreement ensures compliance with the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 (“HIPAA”), the Health Information Technology for Economic and Clinical Health Act (“HITECH”), and all associated regulations.

1. Purpose

Covered Entity and Business Associate enter into this Agreement to ensure that Business Associate will appropriately safeguard Protected Health Information (“PHI”) and Electronic Protected Health Information (“ePHI”) in compliance with federal and state privacy laws.

2. Definitions

  • PHI: Individually identifiable health information transmitted or maintained in any form.
  • ePHI: PHI transmitted or maintained electronically.
  • Business Associate: Contractor who handles PHI when performing services for Covered Entity.
  • HIPAA Rules: HIPAA Privacy, Security, Breach Notification, and Enforcement Rules.

3. Permitted Uses and Disclosures

Business Associate may use or disclose PHI only to:

  • Perform clinical or administrative services for Covered Entity
  • Fulfill contractual duties under the Independent Contractor Agreement
  • Comply with legal requirements

Business Associate may not use PHI for personal benefit, marketing, research, or any purpose not explicitly authorized by Covered Entity or required by law.

4. Safeguards

Business Associate shall implement administrative, physical, and technical safeguards that reasonably protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of PHI and ePHI, including:

  • Password protection and secure authentication
  • Use of encrypted devices and secure networks
  • HIPAA-compliant messaging systems (e.g., Spruce)
  • Access controls limiting PHI to authorized personnel
  • Secure disposal of PHI when authorized
  • Use of multi-factor authentication, where available

5. Minimum Necessary Standard

Business Associate shall use, access, or disclose only the minimum necessary PHI required to perform assigned responsibilities.

6. Reporting Obligations

Business Associate shall immediately report any known or suspected:

  • Unauthorized access, use, or disclosure of PHI
  • Loss or theft of a device containing PHI
  • Improper disposal of PHI
  • Security breach, ransomware event, or system compromise

Reports must be made no later than 48 hours after discovery.

7. Subcontractors

Business Associate shall ensure that any subcontractors who receive PHI agree in writing to the same restrictions and safeguards required by this Agreement.

8. Prohibited Actions

Business Associate shall NOT:

  • Use personal email, SMS, or social media to transmit PHI
  • Store PHI on unencrypted or unauthorized devices
  • Download PHI outside of Company-approved systems
  • Share login credentials or access PHI under another user’s account
  • Transfer PHI outside the United States unless authorized in writing

9. Patient Rights Support

Business Associate shall assist Covered Entity in fulfilling patient rights, including:

  • Access to medical records
  • Requests for amendment
  • Accounting of disclosures

10. Breach Notification

In the event of a breach, Business Associate shall provide Covered Entity with:

  • A summary of what happened
  • Types of PHI involved
  • Names of affected individuals
  • Corrective actions taken to mitigate harm

If required by law, Covered Entity will handle patient notifications unless otherwise agreed in writing.

11. Authorized Systems

Business Associate shall use only the following systems for PHI:

  • EHR platform designated by MTN HLTH
  • Spruce secure messaging
  • Company Google Workspace
  • HIPAA-compliant telemedicine platforms
  • Company-approved internal software and automations

12. Training Requirements

Business Associate shall complete all required HIPAA and cybersecurity training, including updates provided by Covered Entity.

13. Term & Termination

This Agreement remains effective for the full duration of the Contractor’s engagement and terminates only when:

  • All services for Covered Entity have ended
  • All PHI has been returned or destroyed
  • Covered Entity confirms compliance with termination procedures

Covered Entity may terminate this Agreement immediately for any material violation.

14. Return or Destruction of PHI

Upon termination of the underlying Contractor Agreement, Business Associate shall:

  • Return all PHI to Covered Entity, or
  • Destroy PHI using secure, irreversible methods, if authorized
  • Certify in writing that no PHI remains on devices or storage systems

15. Indemnification

Business Associate agrees to indemnify and hold harmless Covered Entity from any losses, damages, penalties, or liabilities resulting from Business Associate’s negligence, unauthorized disclosures, or failure to comply with HIPAA obligations.

16. Governing Law

This Agreement is governed by the laws of the State of Utah and applicable federal laws.

17. Entire Agreement

This Agreement represents the complete understanding of the parties regarding HIPAA compliance and supersedes all prior agreements on this subject.

By signing electronically below, Business Associate agrees to fully comply with this HIPAA Business Associate Agreement.

Telemedicine Compliance Agreement

This Telemedicine Compliance Agreement (“Agreement”) is entered into by and between MTN HLTH (“Company”) and the undersigned Contractor (“Provider”) as of the date of electronic signature. This Agreement outlines Provider’s obligations to comply with federal and state telemedicine laws, clinical standards, technology requirements, and Company-specific telemedicine protocols.

1. Purpose

The purpose of this Agreement is to ensure that Provider delivers safe, compliant, and high-quality care through telemedicine and adheres to all legal and regulatory requirements in the states where Company operates.

2. Provider Licensure Requirements

Provider agrees that:

  • Provider will maintain active licensure in every state where they see patients
  • Provider shall practice only within states where they hold valid licensure
  • Provider shall notify Company immediately of any change in licensure status
  • Provider shall review and comply with each state’s telehealth rules

Provider may not provide services to patients located in a state where Provider is not licensed, regardless of Company’s authorization.

3. Patient Location Verification

Provider must verify and document the patient’s physical location at every telemedicine visit, as required by law.

  • Documentation must include the city and state where the patient is physically located
  • If the patient is out of state, the visit may proceed only if Provider has licensure in that state
  • Providers may not instruct patients to “select a licensed state” unless legally permissible

4. Technology and Privacy Requirements

Provider shall conduct telemedicine visits using the following:

  • HIPAA-compliant telemedicine platform designated by Company
  • Secure internet connection (no public Wi-Fi without VPN)
  • Environment that ensures patient confidentiality (no public spaces)
  • Updated devices with proper privacy settings and security controls

Provider shall NOT:

  • Use FaceTime, Instagram, WhatsApp, Messenger, or other non-compliant apps
  • Conduct patient visits in a non-private or noisy environment
  • Record telemedicine sessions unless specifically approved in writing

5. Clinical Standards & Prescribing Requirements

Provider shall adhere to all national and state prescribing laws, including telemedicine-specific rules relating to controlled substances. Provider agrees to:

  • Perform appropriate evaluation and documentation before prescribing
  • Follow treatment protocols established by Company
  • Comply with the Ryan Haight Act and any applicable exceptions
  • Document clinical reasoning for all prescriptions
  • Use Company-approved pharmacies and e-prescribing systems

Provider shall not prescribe controlled substances unless explicitly permitted and compliant with federal and state law.

6. Documentation Requirements

Provider agrees to:

  • Complete all charting within one (1) business day
  • Record patient location, consent, assessment, and treatment plan
  • Follow Company templates, workflows, and documentation standards
  • Document telehealth modality and technology used for each visit

7. Informed Consent Requirements

Provider must ensure that telemedicine informed consent is obtained, documented, and follows all state-specific telehealth rules.

Consent shall include:

  • Description of telemedicine technology and limitations
  • Potential risks to privacy or data security
  • Right to refuse telemedicine services
  • Identity verification of both Provider and patient

8. Emergency and Safety Protocols

Provider agrees to follow Company’s safety standards, including:

  • Assessing whether telemedicine is appropriate for each visit
  • Documenting emergency contact information each year
  • Knowing the patient’s local emergency services
  • Escalating cases when telemedicine is insufficient

Provider must call 911 or notify local emergency services when clinically indicated and document all steps taken.

9. Prohibited Telemedicine Conduct

Provider shall NOT engage in the following:

  • Providing care outside their scope of practice
  • Prescribing without proper evaluation
  • Using telemedicine to bypass in-person care requirements
  • Engaging in dual relationships or inappropriate conduct with patients
  • Practicing without state licensure or required credentials

10. Reporting Requirements

Provider must report to Company immediately if:

  • The Provider becomes aware of any telehealth-related complaint
  • There is any system outage affecting patient care
  • There is a privacy or PHI breach
  • There is a technology failure affecting documentation or prescribing

11. Quality Assurance & Audits

Company may review Provider’s telemedicine encounters, documentation, and performance for quality improvement, training, and compliance purposes.

  • Provider must cooperate with audits and requests for records
  • Company may provide feedback or require corrective action
  • Failure to comply may result in suspension or termination

12. Termination

Company may terminate this Agreement or restrict Provider’s telemedicine activities for any violation of telemedicine laws, unsafe clinical conduct, or failure to follow Company policies.

13. Governing Law

This Agreement is governed by the laws of the State of Utah and any federal regulations governing telemedicine.

14. Acknowledgment

By signing electronically below, Provider acknowledges that they understand and agree to comply with all telemedicine laws, clinical standards, documentation requirements, and Company protocols outlined in this Agreement.
