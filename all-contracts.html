Compensation Addendum

This Compensation Addendum (“Addendum”) is incorporated into and made part of the Independent Contractor Agreement between MTN HLTH (“Company”) and the undersigned Provider. This Addendum outlines the compensation structure, eligibility requirements, payment schedule, and revenue reconciliation rules.

1. Compensation Structure

Provider shall be compensated using the following revenue-share tiers:

  • 0–50 Active Patients: 50% revenue share
  • 50–100 Active Patients: 55% revenue share
  • 100+ Active Patients: 65% revenue share

Tiers are recalculated monthly based on the number of Active Patients.

2. Definition of an Active Patient

An “Active Patient” is a patient who is enrolled in a recurring MTN HLTH Wellness Plan, whose payment has successfully settled for the billing cycle, and who has not canceled, refunded, or reversed payment during the compensation period.

The following patients are excluded from Active Patient counts:

  • patients with failed charges
  • patients who request refunds
  • patients whose payment is reversed, disputed, or charged back
  • patients whose membership was canceled before settlement

3. Revenue Eligible for Compensation

Provider is compensated only for collected and fully settled revenue actually received by Company. Compensation excludes:

  • pending or unsettled charges
  • failed transactions
  • refunds or partial refunds
  • chargebacks or disputes
  • voided invoices or administrative write-offs

4. Payment Schedule

Compensation shall be paid on the first (1st) business day of each month. This ensures reconciliation of revenue from the standard fifteenth (15th) Wellness Plan billing cycle and provides sufficient time to process cancellations, refunds, and adjustments.

5. Documentation Completion Requirement

Provider must complete all clinical documentation within one (1) business day unless otherwise approved in writing.

Company may withhold payment for:

  • incomplete or missing documentation
  • unsigned or improperly coded notes
  • documentation that fails to meet clinical or compliance standards
  • encounters that cannot legally or safely be billed

6. Refunds, Chargebacks & Adjustments

If revenue previously included in a payout is later refunded, reversed, or charged back, Company may:

  • deduct the amount from Provider’s next payout, or
  • invoice the Provider directly if no future payouts remain.

7. Claw-Back Provision

Company may recover compensation issued in error, including but not limited to amounts paid for reversed revenue, documentation deficiencies, compliance violations, or misattributed revenue.

8. Compensation Period

Each compensation period includes:

  • all revenue billed on or after the fifteenth (15th) of the prior month, and
  • all revenue that fully settles before the next payout on the first (1st).

Revenue outside this window is rolled forward to the next period.

9. No Guarantee of Minimum Compensation

Company does not guarantee a minimum patient load, income level, or number of encounters.

10. Compensation Model Adjustments

Company may modify the revenue-share model, Active Patient definition, or compensation terms with thirty (30) days written notice.

11. Governing Law

This Addendum is governed by Utah law.

12. Acknowledgment

Nothing in this Addendum alters or modifies the termination notice period set forth in Section 11 of the Independent Contractor Agreement.

Provider understands and agrees to the terms of this Compensation Addendum.

Telemedicine Compliance Agreement

This Telemedicine Compliance Agreement (“Agreement”) is entered into by and between MTN HLTH (“Company”) and the undersigned Provider as of the date of electronic signature. This Agreement outlines Provider’s obligations to comply with federal and state telemedicine laws, clinical standards, technology requirements, and Company-specific telemedicine protocols.

1. Purpose

The purpose of this Agreement is to ensure that Provider delivers safe, compliant, and high-quality care through telemedicine and adheres to all legal and regulatory requirements in the states where Company operates.

2. Provider Licensure Requirements

Provider agrees that:

  • Provider will maintain active licensure in every state where they see patients
  • Provider shall practice only within states where they hold valid licensure
  • Provider shall notify Company immediately of any change in licensure status
  • Provider shall review and comply with each state’s telehealth rules

Provider may not provide services to patients located in a state where Provider is not licensed, regardless of Company’s authorization.

3. Patient Location Verification

Provider must verify and document the patient’s physical location at every telemedicine visit, as required by law.

  • Documentation must include the city and state where the patient is physically located
  • If the patient is out of state, the visit may proceed only if Provider has licensure in that state
  • Providers may not instruct patients to “select a licensed state” unless legally permissible

4. Technology and Privacy Requirements

Provider shall conduct telemedicine visits using the following:

  • HIPAA-compliant telemedicine platform designated by Company
  • Secure internet connection (no public Wi-Fi without VPN)
  • Environment that ensures patient confidentiality (no public spaces)
  • Updated devices with proper privacy settings and security controls

Provider shall NOT:

  • Use FaceTime, Instagram, WhatsApp, Messenger, or other non-compliant apps
  • Conduct patient visits in a non-private or noisy environment
  • Record telemedicine sessions unless specifically approved in writing

5. Clinical Standards & Prescribing Requirements

Provider shall adhere to all national and state prescribing laws, including telemedicine-specific rules relating to controlled substances. Provider agrees to:

  • Perform appropriate evaluation and documentation before prescribing
  • Follow treatment protocols established by Company
  • Comply with the Ryan Haight Act and any applicable exceptions
  • Document clinical reasoning for all prescriptions
  • Use Company-approved pharmacies and e-prescribing systems

Provider shall not prescribe controlled substances unless explicitly permitted and compliant with federal and state law.

6. Documentation Requirements

Provider agrees to:

  • Complete all charting within one (1) business day
  • Record patient location, consent, assessment, and treatment plan
  • Follow Company templates, workflows, and documentation standards
  • Document telehealth modality and technology used for each visit

7. Informed Consent Requirements

Provider must ensure that telemedicine informed consent is obtained, documented, and follows all state-specific telehealth rules.

Consent shall include:

  • Description of telemedicine technology and limitations
  • Potential risks to privacy or data security
  • Right to refuse telemedicine services
  • Identity verification of both Provider and patient

8. Emergency and Safety Protocols

Provider agrees to follow Company’s safety standards, including:

  • Assessing whether telemedicine is appropriate for each visit
  • Documenting emergency contact information each year
  • Knowing the patient’s local emergency services
  • Escalating cases when telemedicine is insufficient

Provider must call 911 or notify local emergency services when clinically indicated and document all steps taken.

9. Prohibited Telemedicine Conduct
