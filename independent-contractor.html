Independent Contractor Agreement

This Independent Contractor Agreement (“Agreement”) is entered into by and between MTN HLTH (“Company”) and the undersigned individual (“Contractor”) as of the date of electronic signature. This Agreement governs the terms under which Contractor provides professional clinical and related services to the Company as a 1099 independent contractor.

1. Engagement

Company engages Contractor to provide functional medicine, wellness, and/or mid-level clinical services within Contractor’s scope of practice, and Contractor agrees to perform such services in accordance with applicable laws, ethical standards, and Company policies.

2. Independent Contractor Status

Contractor is an independent contractor and not an employee, agent, partner, or representative of Company. Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed as creating an employment relationship. Contractor is solely responsible for all taxes, insurance, licensure costs, continuing education, and any other business expenses incurred in the performance of services.

Contractor acknowledges that Company does not provide health benefits, retirement benefits, workers’ compensation coverage, unemployment coverage, or any other employment-related benefits.

3. Compensation

Contractor will be compensated according to the rates, revenue-share structure, or percentage-based compensation detailed in the attached Compensation Addendum, or any updated addendum mutually agreed upon in writing. Payments are typically made on a monthly basis following review of completed visits, notes, and billables.

Contractor shall submit invoices when requested and agrees that all compensation received shall be reported to tax authorities as independent contractor income.

4. Duties & Responsibilities

Contractor agrees to perform the following duties:

  • Provide patient care via telemedicine or in-person (if applicable)
  • Maintain accurate and timely clinical documentation
  • Follow functional medicine, hormone optimization, and wellness protocols established by Company
  • Order labs, prescriptions, and treatments within licensure scope and Company standards
  • Use Company systems (EHR, Spruce, Google Workspace, etc.) as directed
  • Respond to patient messages and tasks within expected turnaround times
  • Participate in internal case reviews, team meetings, or training sessions when requested
  • Practice within all federal, state, and local regulations, including telemedicine standards
  • Comply with Company policies, SOPs, and clinical quality expectations

5. Clinical Documentation

Contractor shall complete visit notes, charting, and clinical documentation within one (1) business day unless otherwise approved. Failure to complete timely documentation may result in delayed payment.

6. Tools, Software, and Systems

Company will provide access to its clinical systems, including but not limited to:

  • EHR platform
  • Secure messaging tools (e.g., Spruce)
  • Google Workspace apps
  • Telemedicine platforms
  • Internal templates, workflows, and SOPs

Contractor agrees to use these systems exclusively for Company-related work and to comply with all privacy, security, and acceptable-use requirements.

7. Confidentiality & HIPAA Compliance

Contractor agrees to maintain full confidentiality of all Protected Health Information (PHI) and Company proprietary information. Contractor will sign and comply with the Company’s HIPAA/PHI Confidentiality Agreement, Security Rule Attestation, and Business Associate Agreement (BAA).

Contractor shall not store PHI on personal devices unless encrypted and permitted, and shall use secure platforms designated by Company.

8. Proprietary Information & Intellectual Property

All systems, workflows, algorithms, templates, Google Sheets, GitHub code, internal Wellness Plan structures, automations, scripts, messaging templates, and clinical protocols created by or provided by Company remain the exclusive property of Company.

Contractor shall not reproduce, modify, share, or use Company intellectual property for any purpose outside of this Agreement. Contractor shall not develop or operate a competing clinic or service using Company’s proprietary methods, technology, or clinical frameworks.

9. Non-Solicitation

For a period of two (2) years following termination of this Agreement, Contractor shall not directly or indirectly:

  • Solicit or attempt to solicit Company patients
  • Induce any Company employee or contractor to leave the Company
  • Market competing services to active or former Company patients
  • Use Company patient lists, contacts, marketing funnels, or lead data

This provision shall survive termination.

10. Non-Competition (Limited)

Contractor shall not, for one (1) year following termination, establish or work in a business substantially similar to Company using Company’s proprietary systems, processes, templates, workflows, pricing, or treatment structures.

This clause is intended to protect Company trade secrets and intellectual property and is not intended to restrict Contractor from practicing in their field generally.

11. Term & Termination

This Agreement remains in effect until terminated. Either party may terminate this Agreement with thirty (30) days written notice. Company may terminate immediately for material breach, loss of licensure, gross misconduct, or violations of law.

12. Insurance Requirements

Contractor must maintain professional liability (malpractice) insurance with minimum coverage limits acceptable to the Company and shall provide proof of coverage upon request. Contractor is responsible for any required tail coverage.

13. Licensure & Compliance

Contractor represents and warrants that they hold all licenses, certifications, prescriptive authority, DEA registration (if applicable), and credentials necessary to perform services. Contractor will immediately notify Company of any investigation, restriction, or disciplinary action related to licensure.

14. Indemnification

Contractor agrees to indemnify and hold harmless the Company, its owners, and its affiliates from any claims, damages, or liabilities arising out of Contractor’s actions, omissions, negligence, or violations of law.

15. Records & Data Ownership

All patient records, visit notes, internal communications, and work product produced by Contractor in relation to Company services are the exclusive property of Company.

16. Return of Property

Upon termination, Contractor shall immediately return all Company property, equipment, documents, access credentials, templates, spreadsheets, code, and any other materials, whether digital or physical.

17. Dispute Resolution

Any disputes shall first be addressed through good-faith negotiation. If unresolved, the parties agree to binding arbitration in the state of Utah.

18. Governing Law

This Agreement is governed by the laws of the State of Utah.

19. Entire Agreement

This Agreement, together with all referenced addenda and supplemental agreements, constitutes the entire understanding between the parties and supersedes all prior agreements.

By signing electronically below, Contractor acknowledges that they have read, understand, and agree to be bound by this Agreement in full.
